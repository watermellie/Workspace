## Part 1 â€” Why Visible, *Measured* Impact Wins Return Offers

Three forces are working against you, and the ritual neutralizes all three.

**1. The recency trap.** People remember the last two weeks and forget the first ten. If your strongest work happened in June, it's invisible by August unless it's written down. A weekly log defeats recency by creating a permanent record of *everything*.

**2. The attribution gap.** "I helped with the onboarding redesign" is a sentence about activity. "Onboarding redesign I led cut first-session drop-off from 38% to 24%" is a sentence about *value*. Managers don't fight for activity. They fight for value they can quantify, because numbers survive cross-examination and adjectives don't.

**3. The advocacy handoff.** The person deciding your fate often *isn't* your manager â€” it's a panel your manager has to persuade. You want your manager to be able to copy-paste your wins into their advocacy. Make their argument *for* them, in their language, with the metrics already attached.

> **Mantra:** *Adjectives are claims. Numbers are evidence. Bring evidence.*

The intern who logs measured impact weekly isn't "better at the work" than the one who doesn't. They're better at *making the work legible* â€” and legible work is the work that gets rewarded.

## Part 3 â€” The X-Y-Z Bullet Formula

This is the format Google's own recruiting guidance popularized, and it works because it forces evidence into every line:

> **Accomplished [X], measured by [Y], by doing [Z].**

- **X â€” the accomplishment.** What changed in the world (or the product) because you existed?
- **Y â€” the metric.** The number that proves X is real. This is the part interns skip and the part that wins offers.
- **Z â€” the method.** *How* you did it â€” this is where your skill and judgment show.

**No metric yet? Use a proxy.** Estimated reach, time saved, error count, steps removed, stakeholders aligned, tickets closed. "Reduced the checkout flow from 7 steps to 4" is a metric even if you can't see conversion yet. *Something countable beats nothing.*
